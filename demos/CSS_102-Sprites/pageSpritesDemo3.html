HOVERS: How to avoid the previous example's flaw? By adding a second after state and carefully selecting which objects show where. The image in this case has split the purple and blue shapes into the first after state, and the green, orange and yellow shapes into the second. In other words there is 1 before state and 2 after states. This order allows boxes to be drawn around each objectâ€™s after state without including pieces of the surrounding objects.

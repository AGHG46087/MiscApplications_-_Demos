Offers

Geo Location always Opt-In The geolocation API explicitly states: "User Agents must not send location information to Web sites without the express permission of the user."
    There's a lot going on in the Geo Location InfoBar... You, as the end user,
  • are told which website wants to know your location
  • can choose to share your location
  • can choose not to share your location
  • can tell your browser to remember your choice (either way, share or don't share) so you never see this infobar again on this website
  • unconditional, so there is no way for a website to bypass it
  • blocking, so there is no chance that the website can determine your location while it's waiting for your answer
So What
  • Geolocation: Based on Cell Radio, WiFi radio, Satellite and ISP.
  • Geolocation although great for mobile - also works for Desktop (ISP) and WiFi devices.
  • Geolocation services and offers can be extended to users regardless of device utilized (provided user consent)
  • Offereing a user to enter alternative location extends the benefits to Geolocation.
